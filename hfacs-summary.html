HFACS Framework Summary

I. Unsafe Acts

  • Decision Errors: Misjudgments or flawed planning with good intentions.
  • Skill-Based Errors: Lapses in attention or memory during routine tasks.
  • Perceptual Errors: Misinterpretation due to sensory limitations or degraded environments.
  • Routine Violations: Habitual shortcuts or rule-breaking, often tolerated.
  • Exceptional Violations: Rare and unapproved deviations from rules or procedures.

II. Preconditions for Unsafe Acts

  • Mental States: Fatigue, stress, distraction, or haste.
  • Physiological States: Illness, dehydration, or physical fatigue.
  • Environmental Factors: Poor lighting, noise, or cluttered workspaces.
  • Tools & Technology: Poor design or malfunctioning equipment.
  • Team Dynamics: Communication and coordination failures.
  • Off-Duty Issues: Personal lifestyle factors impacting performance.

III. Supervisory Factors

  • Engagement: Lack of oversight, training, or guidance.
  • Planning: Poor staffing or misaligned work assignments.
  • Problem Correction: Ignored known safety issues or hazards.
  • Integrity: Supervisors breaking or encouraging violation of rules.

IV. Organizational Influences

  • Culture: Prioritizing productivity over safety.
  • Process: Unclear policies or conflicting goals.
  • Resource Management: Lack of qualified staff, tools, or budget.

This is a high-level summary of the HFACS (Human Factors Analysis and Classification System) framework, highlighting its main components for incident analysis and safety management.
